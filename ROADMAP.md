# APT Operations Roadmap

Roadmap dates and owners are assigned during operating reviews. Completion requires objective evidence linked in the relevant issue or pull request.

## Phase 0 — Repository foundation

- Establish controlled directory structure, contribution workflow, templates, CI, and validation utility.
- Define owners and approval authority for operations, quality, calibration, training, and equipment.
- Approve data-classification and retention rules before storing customer or regulated data.

## Phase 1 — Minimum viable operations control

- Populate the equipment and calibration registers with uniquely identified assets.
- Release the first production traveler, inspection record, nonconformance, and calibration procedures.
- Define part/job/lot/serial naming and record-location conventions.
- Train the initial team and verify training evidence.

## Phase 2 — Quality system maturity

- Implement internal audit, corrective action, supplier control, and management-review procedures.
- Add calibration-due and training-due reporting with accountable owners.
- Establish KPI definitions for on-time delivery, first-pass yield, scrap/rework, and calibration compliance.

## Phase 3 — Workflow automation

- Add schema-backed records and immutable event history.
- Generate customer-facing job reports from verified source records.
- Add role-based access, electronic approvals, backups, and restore testing.
- Integrate equipment/measurement data only after interface validation and cybersecurity review.

## Phase 4 — Scale and certification readiness

- Perform a formal gap assessment against chosen customer and regulatory requirements.
- Validate computerized systems and record integrity as required by contract.
- Prepare internal audits, management review, and corrective actions before any certification audit.
